Okay! `train_step` is where the agent actually **learns**. This is the heart of DQN. 🧠

---

**Story first:**

Remember the diary (replay buffer)? 

`train_step` is like the agent sitting down every evening, randomly picking 32 diary entries and asking:

*"Given what I knew that day, was my decision good or bad? Let me update my brain accordingly."*

---

**The learning happens in 4 steps:**

---

**Step 1 — Pick 32 random memories:**
```python
batch = self.memory.sample(self.batch_size)
states, actions, rewards, next_states, dones = zip(*batch)
```
Pull 32 random experiences from the diary. Unpack them into separate lists.

---

**Step 2 — What did my brain PREDICT that day?**
```python
current_q = self.model(states).gather(1, actions.unsqueeze(1))
```
Ask the brain — *"for these 32 states, what Q-value did you give for the action we actually took?"*

---

**Step 3 — What SHOULD the Q-value have been?**
```python
next_q = self.target_model(next_states).max(1)[0].detach()
target_q = rewards + self.gamma * next_q * (1 - dones)
```

The correct Q-value = **reward we got** + **best future reward possible**

Like saying: *"that buy decision was worth 2% profit today PLUS whatever good things came after"*

---

**Step 4 — Calculate mistake and fix brain:**
```python
loss = nn.MSELoss()(current_q.squeeze(), target_q)
self.optimizer.zero_grad()
loss.backward()
self.optimizer.step()
```

- `MSELoss` = measures how wrong the prediction was
- `loss.backward()` = figures out which weights caused the mistake
- `optimizer.step()` = adjusts the weights to be less wrong next time

---

**Yes — batch size, loss function, optimizer are all here!** Just like TensorFlow! Same concepts, PyTorch style. 😄

---

31 is our **state size** — the number of price values the agent sees.

32 is our **batch size** — the number of diary entries we learn from at once.

These are two completely different things!

---

**Simple analogy:**

- **31** = how many questions are on one exam paper 📝
- **32** = how many exam papers you study at once 📚

You study 32 papers, each paper has 31 questions.

---

Batch size 32 is actually a standard default in deep learning — not specific to our project. You'll see it everywhere. Could be 16, 32, 64, 128 — always powers of 2. We chose 32 as a safe middle ground. 😄
